# Diligence Checklist

Use this checklist to decide which workstreams to cover. Skip irrelevant items explicitly.

## Business and Market

- Clarify the product, buyer, user, and problem solved.
- Test whether the market is large enough and accessible enough for the claimed outcome.
- Identify the real competitors, including substitutes and in-house alternatives.
- Check whether differentiation is durable or just a temporary feature lead.
- Evaluate pricing logic, packaging, and evidence of willingness to pay.

## Product and Technology

- Review the current product, not just the pitch deck.
- Check whether core claims are visible in docs, demos, reviews, or usage evidence.
- Identify integration burden, switching costs, implementation time, and user friction.
- Assess reliability, outages, security posture, and platform dependence.
- Pressure-test AI or automation claims for where the hard work actually happens.

## Commercial Traction

- Look for proof of real customers, not just logos.
- Assess retention, repeat usage, expansion, or replacement risk.
- Note whether growth appears sales-led, product-led, channel-led, or founder-led.
- Check for customer concentration and dependence on lighthouse accounts.
- Compare growth claims against hiring, support load, and public customer evidence.

## Financial Quality

- Separate recurring from non-recurring revenue.
- Check margin quality and whether services or incentives are masking economics.
- Assess burn, runway, debt, and need for near-term financing.
- Examine working-capital stress, seasonality, and exposure to delayed collections.
- Look for adjustments that make profitability or growth look cleaner than it is.

## Legal and Compliance

- Confirm IP ownership and key licenses.
- Check litigation, disputes, and regulatory scrutiny.
- Review privacy, data-handling, and sector-specific compliance posture.
- Identify export-control, sanctions, labor, or contractor-classification risk where relevant.
- Flag missing policies or weak disclosure in regulated environments.

## Management and Governance

- Review founder or executive track record relative to the current challenge.
- Note leadership turnover, board composition, and investor control dynamics.
- Assess whether incentives align with durable value creation.
- Look for signs of strong operating cadence or weak internal control.
- Test whether management communication is specific, consistent, and falsifiable.

## Deal-Specific Checks

### Investment

- Ask whether the upside is large enough relative to the failure modes.
- Identify the milestones needed for the next round or liquidity event.

### Acquisition

- Evaluate integration difficulty, key-person retention, customer overlap, and synergies.
- Check whether reported performance survives purchase-accounting and standalone-cost adjustments.

### Vendor or Partner

- Focus on security, uptime, roadmap dependence, termination rights, and switching costs.

### Employment

- Focus on business durability, role clarity, leadership quality, compensation realism, and downside risk.
